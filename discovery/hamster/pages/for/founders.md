The problem

## You have the vision. You need the execution layer.

### Your team adopted AI tools but velocity did not change

You invested in Claude Code, Codex, or Cursor expecting faster shipping. But the bottleneck was never typing speed — it was translating product decisions into clear engineering specs. Agents without direction deliver the wrong thing fast.

### Sprint planning, grooming, and story points are gone — but nothing replaced them

Those ceremonies existed to keep your team in sync on what to build and who is doing it. Your team dropped them when AI sped up the work, but now nobody is aligned. You need a new way to set direction.

### Hiring is expensive and slow

Every additional engineer costs $150K+ and takes months to onboard. AI tools promise to multiply output, but only if the team can feed them well-structured work. Without that, you are paying for AI tokens that produce throwaway code.

## Hamster turns your vision into execution.

Describe what you want to build. Hamster AI structures it into a brief with requirements and acceptance criteria. Generate a task plan. Point AI agents at each task and ship.

### Vision to spec in minutes

Founders need to translate vision into executable specs quickly. Hamster briefs let you capture product intent in a structured format that flows directly to engineering and AI tools — no multi-step translation required.

### Auto-generated delivery plans

Founders rarely have time for granular task management. Hamster auto-decomposes briefs into engineering tasks, giving you visibility into scope and timeline without requiring you to do the decomposition.

### Ship like a team twice your size

For founder-led teams using AI coding tools, Hamster is the orchestration layer. It takes the brief and executes it through AI agents, letting small teams punch above their weight without adding headcount.

## How founders use Hamster

### Vision to shipped feature

Before

Describe the feature in a meeting. PM writes a spec. Engineering decomposes it into tasks. Tasks get implemented. Feature ships 3 weeks later — and it is not quite what you described.

After

Write a Hamster brief capturing the feature intent. Review the auto-generated task breakdown in 10 minutes. Engineering and AI tools execute against the spec. Feature ships in days, matching your vision.
