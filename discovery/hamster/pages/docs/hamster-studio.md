# Hamster Studio

Hamster Studio is the workspace where [Direction](https://tryhamster.com/docs/concepts/direction), [Discovery](https://tryhamster.com/docs/concepts/discovery), [Delivery](https://tryhamster.com/docs/concepts/delivery), and [Knowledge](https://tryhamster.com/docs/concepts/knowledge) stay connected. Product, design, engineering, and AI agents work from the same context so work can move from roughly right to shippable.

## Why context matters

AI coding tools often get the first 70% right: a plausible implementation, a useful draft, a [Plan](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/plans) that mostly fits. The last 30% is where teams usually pay the tax — missing customer context, stale assumptions, unclear ownership, and rework after review.

Hamster is the shared layer that helps humans and AI converge on 99%. A [Brief](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/briefs) says what is being built and why. [Blueprints](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/blueprints) say what is true today. [Methods](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/skills-methods) say how your team works. [Goals](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/goals) and [Metrics](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/metrics) say what the work is meant to move. The [Context Graph](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/context-graph) keeps all of it connected to the systems your team already uses.

## Direction — where you're heading

[Direction](https://tryhamster.com/docs/concepts/direction) is how Hamster connects work to outcomes. [Goals](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/goals) carry the [Metrics](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/metrics) your team cares about, in the framework you already use (OKR, North Star, OGSM, V2MOM, AARRR, HEART). [Results](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/results) track per-period progress, so reviews start from live data tied to the work in flight.

## Discovery — figuring out what to do

[Discovery](https://tryhamster.com/docs/concepts/discovery) is where ambiguity becomes a [Brief](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/briefs). [Hamster Chat](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/chat) gives the team and AI one persistent canvas for questions, drafts, files, and decisions. The [Context Graph](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/context-graph) does the lookup behind every reply, and [Research Agents](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/research-agents) run deeper passes when a quick conversation is not enough.

## Delivery — what you ship

[Delivery](https://tryhamster.com/docs/concepts/delivery) turns aligned context into shipped work. [Briefs](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/briefs) become [Plans](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/plans), [Plans](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/plans) become [Tasks](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/tasks), and [Tasks](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/tasks) become PRs. Group related [Briefs](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/briefs) under [Initiatives](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/initiatives) so priorities ladder up to [Goals](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/goals). [Cloud Agents](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/cloud-agents) ship hands-off, while [CLI](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/cli) and [MCP Server](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/mcp) bring the same context into an engineer's IDE.

## Knowledge — what Hamster reads from

[Knowledge](https://tryhamster.com/docs/concepts/knowledge) is the foundation that keeps AI from drifting between loops. The [Context Graph](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/context-graph) connects Hamster's English layer to your real systems. [Blueprints](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/blueprints) capture what is true today. [Methods](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/skills-methods) capture how your team works. [Connections](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/connections) plug Hamster into GitHub, Linear, Jira, Slack, Figma, Notion, and Google Drive.

## Surfaces and Workspace

Use [Hamster Chat](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/chat) when you are shaping work, [Cloud Agents](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/cloud-agents) when you want hands-off [Delivery](https://tryhamster.com/docs/concepts/delivery), and the [CLI](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/cli) or [MCP Server](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/mcp) when engineers want control in their existing tools. Manage [Teams](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/teams), [Collaboration](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/collaboration), and [Account Settings](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/account-settings) from the Workspace section.

## Getting started

[Getting Started](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/getting-started) walks the first ten minutes: connect a tool or two, open [Hamster Chat](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/chat), shape a [Brief](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/briefs), and ship.
