# Voice mode

_Talk to the AI assistant hands-free using a keyboard shortcut — ask questions, check your [Tasks](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/tasks), and update your work without touching the keyboard to type._

_This feature may need to be enabled for your workspace._

## Overview

Voice lets you interact with Hamster Studio's AI assistant by speaking rather than typing. Hold a keyboard shortcut to talk, release to send your audio, and the assistant responds out loud. The session stays open so you can keep the conversation going across multiple turns. Voice is built for situations where typing is inconvenient: stepping through a [Brief](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/briefs) while on a call, reviewing [Tasks](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/tasks) away from your desk, or running through a checklist hands-free.

The assistant in voice mode is conversational. It greets you, answers questions naturally, and responds in short spoken sentences. Voice requests are routed through the same orchestration pipeline as chat — the assistant decides whether to answer directly, search your workspace, or delegate to a specialist based on what you asked. If you opened the voice panel from a [Brief](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/briefs), the assistant starts grounded in that [Brief](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/briefs); navigate to a different [Brief](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/briefs) in the same session and the context follows you.

## How It Works

1. Hold the shortcut to start talking — Press and hold `Cmd+Shift+S` (Mac) or `Ctrl+Shift+S` (Windows/Linux). The microphone activates and a small indicator appears at the bottom of the screen showing that Hamster is listening. The session connects automatically on your first press.
2. Speak your request — While holding the shortcut, say what you want. Ask a question, request a [Task](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/tasks) list, ask for the status of your briefs. Keep your request focused — one sentence or short phrase works best.
3. Release to send — Let go of the shortcut. The audio is sent and the assistant processes your request. A "Speaking…" indicator appears while the response plays back through your speakers or headphones.
4. Continue the conversation — After the assistant finishes, hold the shortcut again to speak your follow-up. The session stays active for 30 seconds of idle time before automatically disconnecting.

## Interaction Modes

Voice supports two modes:

**Push-to-talk (default)**: Hold `Cmd+Shift+S` while you speak, release when done. This gives you precise control over when the assistant is listening. Recommended for use in noisy environments or shared spaces.

**Continuous**: Press `Cmd+Shift+Space` to activate, speak freely, and press again to stop. The assistant uses automatic speech detection to determine when you've finished a sentence. Better for longer, more conversational interactions in a quiet environment.

## What You Can Do With Voice

The voice assistant understands natural language and can take action on your workspace:

* **List your [Briefs](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/briefs)**: "Show me my [Briefs](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/briefs)" or "What projects do I have?" The assistant reads back your most recent [Briefs](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/briefs) and their status.
* **Connect to a [Brief](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/briefs)**: "Connect to the onboarding redesign brief." This sets the voice session's context so that follow-up questions about [Tasks](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/tasks) reference the right project.
* **List and check [Tasks](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/tasks)**: "What are my [Tasks](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/tasks)?" or "Show me the in-progress tasks." Once connected to a [Brief](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/briefs), the assistant lists [Tasks](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/tasks) by status.
* **Search your [Knowledge](https://tryhamster.com/docs/concepts/knowledge) base**: "Search for our authentication approach" searches your team's documents, [Blueprints](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/blueprints), and [Methods](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/skills-methods) and reads back the most relevant findings.
* **Generate a [Plan](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/plans)**: "Generate a [Plan](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/plans) for this brief." Starts [Task](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/tasks) generation for the connected brief. The [Tasks](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/tasks) appear in the [Brief](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/briefs) while the conversation continues.
* **Ask Hamster**: Ask open-ended questions about your workspace, your team's processes, or your project context. The assistant draws on the same [Knowledge](https://tryhamster.com/docs/concepts/knowledge) base as the chat interface — [Blueprints](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/blueprints), [Methods](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/skills-methods), connected tools, and [Brief](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/briefs) history.
* **Look up recent conversation**: "What did we discuss earlier in this thread?" retrieves recent chat history from the current thread.

## What to Expect

The voice assistant is designed to stay conversational. It responds in one or two short sentences — it doesn't read out long lists or markdown-formatted content. When you ask for a [Task](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/tasks) list with many items, it summarizes and highlights the most relevant ones.

The assistant only calls tools when you explicitly ask for something. If there's a pause in the audio or your request is unclear, it responds with a [Brief](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/briefs) acknowledgment ("I'm here") rather than guessing what you wanted.

A floating indicator at the bottom center of the screen shows the current state:

* Spinning icon — connecting
* Pulsing red microphone — listening
* Pulsing blue speaker — assistant is responding
* Error text — something went wrong (try again with the shortcut)

## Tips

* Start by connecting to the [Brief](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/briefs) you're working on. Say "Connect to \[Brief name\]" to set context, then ask about [Tasks](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/tasks) in that brief.
* Use push-to-talk in meetings or noisy environments. The shortcut gives you control over exactly when the microphone is open.
* Keep requests short and direct. "What are my to-do [Tasks](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/tasks)?" works better than a long compound question.
* If the assistant mishears you, hold the shortcut and try again. Voice sessions are stateful — the context from your earlier turns in the session carries forward.
* Grant microphone access when your browser prompts for it. Voice will not activate without microphone permission.
* If voice doesn't respond, check that the feature is enabled for your workspace and that your browser has microphone access granted for the Hamster Studio domain.
