# Organizing Blueprints

Build a clear document hierarchy, navigate between [Blueprints](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/blueprints) quickly, and keep your organizational [Knowledge](https://tryhamster.com/docs/concepts/knowledge) structured as it grows.

## Overview

[Blueprints](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/blueprints) are organized as a tree of documents. Each workspace has one root-level entry point, and every other document is a child — or grandchild — of an existing one. The navigation panel on the left side of the editor displays the full tree and gives you direct access to every document in your [Blueprint](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/blueprints) library. As your content grows, you can rearrange the structure by moving documents to different parents, or keep the sidebar collapsed for a distraction-free editing experience.

![Blueprint navigation tree showing a hierarchical document structure with nested sections](https://tryhamster.com/_site/images/features/blueprints/organizing-blueprints/blueprints-organized-desktop-light.webp?v=6)![Blueprint navigation tree showing a hierarchical document structure with nested sections](https://tryhamster.com/_site/images/features/blueprints/organizing-blueprints/blueprints-organized-desktop-dark.webp?v=6) 

## How It Works

1. See the full tree — The navigation panel shows all your [Blueprint](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/blueprints) documents in a collapsible tree. Root documents appear at the top. Child documents are nested beneath their parents and can be expanded or collapsed individually.
2. Navigate between documents — Click any document in the tree to open it. The editor switches to that document immediately without a full page reload. Your scroll position and the state of the tree persist across navigation.
3. Expand and collapse branches — Documents with children show a chevron. Click it to expand or collapse that branch. Collapsed branches still navigate correctly — clicking the document title opens it even when the children are hidden.
4. Move a document — To change where a document sits in the hierarchy, use drag-and-drop in the navigation tree. Drag a document node and drop it onto the new parent. The tree updates immediately and the change saves automatically.
5. Delete a document — Hover over a document in the tree to reveal its context menu (the three-dot icon). Select Delete to remove it. If the document has children, they are removed along with it. You will be redirected to the parent document or to the [Blueprints](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/blueprints) list if the deleted document was a root.
6. Collapse the navigation panel — Click the collapse toggle in the document header to hide the tree sidebar. In this mode, the sidebar shrinks to a narrow icon strip. Click the toggle again to expand it back to full width. Your preference is remembered across sessions.

## Key Capabilities

* **Hierarchical tree navigation**: All [Blueprint](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/blueprints) documents are visible in a single panel. The tree supports unlimited nesting depth. Folder icons indicate documents with children; file icons indicate leaf documents.
* **Drag-and-drop reordering**: Move any document to a different position in the tree, including changing its parent. The order within a level reflects creation order by default.
* **Breadcrumb context**: When you open a deeply nested document, breadcrumbs at the top of the editor show the full path from the root. Clicking any breadcrumb item navigates to that ancestor.
* **Real-time tree updates**: If a teammate creates, renames, or deletes a [Blueprint](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/blueprints) while you have the section open, the navigation tree updates automatically. You do not need to refresh.
* **Collapsible sidebar**: The navigation panel can be toggled between full-width and icon-only modes. On screens narrower than 1280px, it collapses automatically to give more space to the document content.
* **Inline create from the tree**: Hover over any document in the navigation tree and click the plus icon to create a child document directly beneath it. The new document opens immediately with its title focused for editing.
* **Delete from context menu**: The three-dot menu on each tree item includes a Delete option, so you can remove documents without opening them first.

## Structuring Your Blueprint

There is no required structure. The right organization depends on what your team needs to document. A few patterns that work well:

* **Flat top-level documents**: One document per major topic (Vision, Strategy, Personas, Voice & Tone). Works well when topics are relatively independent.
* **Topic with sub-sections**: A root document per domain, with child documents for each sub-topic. For example, "Product Strategy" as a root, with "Current Quarter", "Roadmap", and "Principles" as children.
* **Reference library**: A mix of evergreen documents (Company Mission, Personas) and frequently updated ones (Current Focus, OKRs). Both types live in the same tree.

## Tips

* Keep the top level of the tree shallow. Two to four root-level sections is usually enough. Deeper hierarchies are better represented as nested children rather than more roots.
* Name documents with enough specificity to be scannable in the tree. "Q1 Strategy" is easier to find at a glance than "Strategy Notes".
* Use the collapse toggle when writing long documents. Hiding the tree gives more horizontal space to the editor content.
* The AI references all your [Blueprint](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/blueprints) content regardless of how it is structured. You do not need to put everything in one document — a well-organized tree works just as well.
