# Results

_[Results](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/results) are where measurement meets time. Each period in your framework’s cadence (quarter, half, month, sprint — whatever the template uses) gets a target and an actual per measurable [Goal](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/goals), so you can see whether you’re ahead, behind, or off course without exporting a spreadsheet._

## Overview

A **[Result](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/results)** in Hamster is the per-period row for a measurable goal: the **target** you intended, the **actual** you achieved (or the rolled-up value from connected systems), a **status** (On Track, At Risk, Off Track, Achieved, Missed), and optional **confidence** (0–1) when the team’s still forecasting.

[Results](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/results) always use the [Metric](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/metrics) defined on that goal — same unit, [Direction](https://tryhamster.com/docs/concepts/direction), and aggregation — so “35% vs 40%” means the same thing in stand-ups, in the Goals view, and in chat.

## Periods and cadence

Your active [Goals](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/goals) framework instance has a **cadence**. Hamster generates **periods** from it — e.g. Q1 2026, H1 2026, April 2026, or continuous slices for always-on growth frameworks. Each measurable goal gets a [Result](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/results) row per period. You can log targets and actuals as you go; at review time, history is already there.

## What updates results

* Manual entry — Owners with **`goals.manage`** record or adjust target, actual, status, and confidence for a period.
* Roll-ups from [Delivery](https://tryhamster.com/docs/concepts/delivery) — As [Briefs](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/briefs) ship and [Initiatives](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/initiatives) move, Hamster can surface signals that feed goal health; over time, connected analytics and product data will tighten the link between shipped work and the numbers (where your workspace has integrations configured).

When an actual lands outside the band you care about, status and optional alerts make “off track” visible without someone curating a deck.

## Goals view and chat

[Hamster Chat](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/chat) reads the same [Results](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/results) when you ask whether a goal is on track or what moved last period. The Goals experience shows current period and trend: which [Metrics](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/metrics) are green, which need attention, and which goals still have no [Initiatives](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/initiatives) behind them.

## Roles and permissions

Recording and editing [Results](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/results) requires **`goals.manage`**, consistent with [Goals](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/goals) and [Metrics](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/metrics). Members and Reviewers typically see [Results](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/results) read-only. [Roles & Permissions](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/teams/roles-permissions) define access.
