# Billing

_View your team's current [Plan](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/plans), start or change a subscription, and manage payment details._

_This feature may need to be enabled for your workspace._

## Overview

Billing in Hamster Studio is managed at the team level. Each team has its own subscription, [Plan](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/plans), and payment history. Team members with the `billing.manage` permission can access the Billing page, view the current [Plan](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/plans), start a new subscription, and open the billing portal to update payment [Methods](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/skills-methods) or download invoices. Members without this permission see a notice that they cannot manage billing.

## How It Works

1. Open billing — In your team's settings, select Billing. The URL pattern is `/home/[your-team]/billing`. Only team members with billing management permissions can access this page.
2. Choose a [Plan](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/plans) — If your team has no active subscription, the [Plan](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/plans) picker displays the available plans. Select the [Plan](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/plans) and billing interval (monthly or annual) that fits your team, then proceed to checkout.
3. Complete checkout — After selecting a [Plan](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/plans), an embedded checkout form opens. Enter your payment details and confirm. Once payment is processed, your subscription activates immediately.
4. View your current subscription — If your team already has an active subscription, the page shows the current [Plan](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/plans) name, pricing, and billing interval. Lifetime orders (if applicable) display in a separate card.
5. Access the billing portal — If you have a billing management permission and a billing record exists for your team, a "Manage billing" option opens the billing portal in a new window. From there you can update your payment [Method](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/skills-methods), view past invoices, change your [Plan](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/plans), or cancel your subscription.

Hamster Studio offers three [Plan](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/plans) tiers for teams:

| -------------------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------- |
| Free                                                     | For individuals exploring Hamster Studio                                | 1             |
| Team                                                     | For teams shipping with shared ownership                                | 5 (bundled)   |

The Team [Plan](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/plans) is available on monthly ($50/month) or annual ($500/year) billing. Enterprise pricing is available on request. All [Plans](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/plans) include unlimited viewer seats.

## Permissions

Billing access is gated by the `billing.manage` permission within your team. If you need access to billing but cannot see the page, ask a team owner to grant you the billing management role. Team admins are granted billing management by default.

Only the team owner can change team ownership. Billing access does not require ownership — it is a separate permission that owners can grant to other members.

## Brief Quota Notifications

When your team hits its monthly [Brief](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/briefs) cap, every member with the `billing.manage` permission gets a transactional email letting them know — once per period, not per attempt. The email includes a one-click link straight to the team's billing page so the right person can decide on an upgrade.

A few details worth knowing:

* **Team-scoped, not user-scoped.** The teammate who triggered the cap may not be the person who can upgrade. The notification goes to whoever holds `billing.manage`, not the person who hit the wall.
* **Once per cycle.** Concurrent attempts collapse into a single email. Two engineers hitting the cap at the same time produces one notification, not two.
* **Cleared automatically.** Upgrading the subscription, completing checkout, or rolling into a new monthly cycle resets the notification — the next time the team hits the cap, it fires again. You don't need to clear anything manually.
* **Doesn't block creation.** If the email service is briefly unavailable, [Brief](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/briefs) creation still returns the upgrade modal to the user who tried. The notification is best-effort; the paywall response is the canonical signal.

## Tips

* Annual billing saves roughly two months compared to monthly billing on the Team plan. Switch to annual in the billing portal at any time to apply the discount at your next renewal.
* If your team is trialing a [Plan](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/plans), the trial eligibility is based on whether your team has previously been a paying customer. First-time teams may see a trial option during checkout.
* To cancel a subscription or get a refund, open the billing portal and follow the cancellation flow. Cancellations take effect at the end of the current billing period.
* Make sure at least one team member with `billing.manage` has a deliverable email on their profile. The cap notification skips members without one — and a team with no reachable billing manager won't get the email at all.
