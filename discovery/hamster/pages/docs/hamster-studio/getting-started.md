# Getting Started

_Sign up at [tryhamster.com](https://tryhamster.com). Once you're in, the path below tends to get teams to real value the fastest._

## 1\. Get familiar with the concepts

Hamster Studio helps you structure ideas, products, and the way your team operates — through Goals, [Initiatives](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/initiatives), [Briefs](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/briefs), [Blueprints](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/blueprints), [Methods](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/skills-methods), and the Context Graph. Every feature you'll use maps to one of those, so the rest of the product makes a lot more sense once the words are familiar. The [Concepts](https://tryhamster.com/docs/concepts) section walks through them in about five minutes — it's worth doing before you go much further.

## 2\. Populate your Context Graph

Hamster is most useful when it can read the work your team has already accumulated, so the next thing worth doing is connecting the tools you already use.

Most teams find [GitHub](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/connections/github) and [Slack](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/connections/slack) are the two highest-leverage [Connections](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/connections) to start with — GitHub because it's the largest single source of code-level context, Slack because it's where most of your team's running conversation already lives. From there, add [Linear](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/connections/linear) or [Jira](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/connections/jira), [Figma](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/connections/figma), [Notion](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/connections/notion), [Google Drive](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/connections/google-drive), and the [Meeting Agent](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/connections/meeting-agent) as the need comes up.

Each [Connection](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/connections) widens what the AI can read when it works on a [Brief](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/briefs), generates a [Plan](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/plans), or proposes an initiative. Two [Connections](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/connections) is plenty to begin with; the rest tend to grow in naturally as you use Hamster more.

## 3\. Draft your first blueprints

A [Blueprint](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/blueprints) is the English description of something stable in your business — a product, a system, a team. The easiest way to start is to chat with Hamster about what you'd like a [Blueprint](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/blueprints) for:

> _"Build me a [Blueprint](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/blueprints) for our checkout product. The repo is `acme/checkout`. It's used by first-time buyers."_

Hamster reads what's already in your [Context Graph](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/context-graph), drafts a [Blueprint](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/blueprints), and hands you back something concrete to react to and edit. You can repeat this for the systems and surfaces that matter most — three or four [Blueprints](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/blueprints) covering your highest-traffic products is usually plenty to ground every [Brief](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/briefs) that follows.

## 4\. Take an idea from hunch to delivery

The fastest way to feel what Hamster actually does is to run an idea you've been kicking around through the whole loop, end-to-end.

### Try it on your own first

A first run usually looks like this:

1. Start a chat about the idea — conversationally is fine, no need to write a formal spec.
2. Ask Hamster to turn it into a brief. It drafts one grounded in the relevant [Blueprints](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/blueprints) and the context already in the graph.
3. Refine the [Brief](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/briefs) in chat. Push back, clarify scope, add what was missing the first time around.
4. Generate a plan. The [Brief](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/briefs) becomes a structured [Task](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/tasks) breakdown.
5. Hand it off to a [Cloud Agent](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/cloud-agents), which picks up the [Plan](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/plans) and ships a PR.

Doing this once on your own first lets you feel the loop — chat, [Brief](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/briefs), [Plan](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/plans), delivered — before you bring anyone else into it.

### Then bring your team in

Once the loop feels familiar, run it again with other people in the room. [Invite teammates](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/teams/inviting-members) — Reviewer seats are free and unlimited, which makes them the right default for stakeholders who only need to read [Briefs](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/briefs) and vote on alignment.

The flow looks the same as solo: share a [Brief](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/briefs), gather feedback, collect alignment votes, and deliver once everyone has signed off. The [Blueprints](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/blueprints) and [Briefs](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/briefs) you've already built carry full context, so teammates joining a discussion can pick it up without needing a half-hour catch-up first.

## 5\. Engineers: pull context into your existing tools

If you ship code, you don't have to leave your editor. Connecting the [CLI](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/cli) or the [MCP Server](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/mcp) lets your IDE — Cursor, Claude Code, or anything else that speaks MCP — pull a [Brief](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/briefs), its [Plan](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/plans), the relevant [Blueprints](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/blueprints), and the connected context straight into your session. The [Brief](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/briefs) becomes the prompt; your [Context Graph](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/context-graph) becomes the grounding; you write the code.

[Cloud Agents](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/cloud-agents) are the right call when you want hands-off delivery. The [CLI](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/cli) or MCP fit better when you want full control with full context. Both paths read from the same [Briefs](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/briefs), [Plans](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/plans), and [Blueprints](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/blueprints), so you can pick whichever suits the work — and switch on a per-brief basis.
