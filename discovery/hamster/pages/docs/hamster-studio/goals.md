# Goals

Pick the framework your team already uses — OKR, OGSM, V2MOM, AARRR, HEART, or North Star — and Hamster gives you the structure to track what you want and link it to the work that delivers it.

[Metrics](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/metrics) is where you define how each measurable goal is counted — unit, [Direction](https://tryhamster.com/docs/concepts/direction), aggregation, baselines, and targets. [Results](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/results) is where per-period targets and actuals land, with status and confidence, so the scoreboard stays current without a parallel spreadsheet.

## Overview

Goals are how your team captures **what you want** in a place every teammate, every [Brief](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/briefs), and the AI assistant can read. Hamster ships with six measurement frameworks built in. You pick the one your team works with — Hamster enforces the shape, you fill in the substance.

Each framework template defines:

* The hierarchy — what kinds of goals it has, what nests under what, how many of each.
* The default cadence — how often you review (quarterly, annual, monthly, or continuous).
* Which goals are measurable — where you can attach [Metrics](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/metrics), targets, baselines, and per-period results.

Goals are the layer that sits above [Initiatives](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/initiatives). [Initiatives](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/initiatives) are the bundles of work that deliver a goal; [Briefs](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/briefs) sit inside initiatives. So the flow goes Goals → [Initiatives](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/initiatives) → [Briefs](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/briefs) → [Plans](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/plans) → [Tasks](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/tasks) → PRs — outcome at the top, shipped change at the bottom.

You can switch frameworks later. Switching doesn't disturb the work below — your [Initiatives](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/initiatives), [Briefs](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/briefs), [Plans](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/plans), and [Tasks](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/tasks) all stay intact.

## The frameworks Hamster ships with

| Framework  | Hierarchy you fill in                                                                                                                             | Default cadence | When it fits                                                                                               |
| ---------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | --------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| OKR        | Objective → 2–5 Key [Results](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/results)                                                                 | Quarterly       | Most product and engineering teams; familiar from Google/Intel                                             |
| OGSM       | Objective → 2–5 Goals → Strategies → Measures                                                                                                     | Annual          | Leadership teams that want a single page from objective down to measurement                                |
| V2MOM      | Vision · Values · [Methods](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/skills-methods) · Obstacles · Measures                                     | Annual          | Salesforce-style framework that names what could derail you, not just what you're chasing                  |
| AARRR      | Acquisition / Activation / Retention / Referral / Revenue → [Metrics](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/metrics)                         | Continuous      | Growth and lifecycle teams measuring funnel movement                                                       |
| HEART      | Feature → Happiness/Engagement/Adoption/Retention/Task Success → Goals · Signals · [Metrics](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/metrics)  | Continuous      | Feature- or surface-level UX measurement                                                                   |
| North Star | North Star [Metric](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/metrics) → 3–5 Input [Metrics](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/metrics) | Continuous      | Product-led teams centred on a single primary [Metric](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/metrics) |

Each framework template is versioned and curated by Hamster. When templates evolve, your existing goals don't break — your framework instance keeps its current shape until you choose to upgrade.

## How it works

1. Pick a framework — From the Goals page, create a framework instance from a template. Give it a name (e.g. "Q1 2026 OKRs", "Growth AARRR"), pick a cadence (quarterly, annual, monthly, continuous), and optionally set a start and end date.
2. Fill in the hierarchy — Each framework has its own goal types. OKR has Objectives and Key Results. OGSM has Objectives, Goals, Strategies, and Measures. V2MOM has all five top-level types. Hamster enforces the shape — you can't put a Key [Result](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/results) under a Strategy or skip a layer.
3. [Metrics](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/metrics) — On any goal type marked measurable (Key [Results](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/results), OGSM Measures, AARRR/HEART [Metrics](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/metrics), North Star and Input [Metrics](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/metrics)), attach a unit, a [Direction](https://tryhamster.com/docs/concepts/direction) (increase / decrease / maintain), an aggregation (sum / avg / last / max / min), an optional baseline, and a target. OGSM Measures can additionally be tagged as **leading** or **lagging** KPIs.
4. [Results](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/results) — A framework's cadence creates **periods** (Q1 2026, H1 2026, etc.). For each measurable goal, log a target and an actual per period, plus a status (On Track, At Risk, Off Track, Achieved, Missed) and an optional confidence (0–1).
5. Link [Initiatives](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/initiatives) to goals — From an [Initiative](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/initiatives), link it to the goal(s) it's meant to deliver. A single [Initiative](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/initiatives) can contribute to multiple goals; a goal can be delivered by multiple initiatives. Optionally weight each link (0–1) when an [Initiative](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/initiatives) only partly serves a goal.
6. Watch work ladder up — As [Briefs](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/briefs) ship, their [Initiatives](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/initiatives) roll up into goals. The Goals view shows you which goals have [Initiatives](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/initiatives) behind them, which are unanchored, and which are tracking against their targets.

## Goals in chat

The AI assistant reads from your goals when it refines [Briefs](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/briefs), generates [Plans](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/plans), and proposes [Initiatives](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/initiatives), alongside [Blueprints](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/blueprints) and the [Context Graph](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/context-graph). Ask things like:

* _"What goals does our Q1 OKR framework have?"_
* _"Which [Initiatives](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/initiatives) are linked to the Activation goal?"_
* _"Are any active goals off track right now?"_
* _"Suggest an [Initiative](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/initiatives) to move Activation from 30% to 60%."_

When the assistant proposes a [Brief](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/briefs) or an [Initiative](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/initiatives), it can reference the goal it ladders up to — so the why is visible at every layer.

## Switching frameworks without breaking delivery

Frameworks are deliberately separated from delivery. Your [Initiatives](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/initiatives), [Briefs](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/briefs), [Plans](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/plans), and [Tasks](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/tasks) don't depend on which framework you've chosen. If your team moves from OKRs to OGSM, you change the framework instance — the work below stays exactly where it is, and the new framework's goals can re-link to the same initiatives.

This is intentional: frameworks are how you measure direction. [Delivery](https://tryhamster.com/docs/concepts/delivery) shouldn't have to change shape every time the team picks a new measurement language.

## Roles and permissions

Managing goals (creating frameworks, editing goals, attaching [Metrics](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/metrics), recording [Results](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/results)) requires the **`goals.manage`** permission. By default this is held by Owners and Admins; Members and Reviewers see goals read-only. [Roles and permissions](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/teams/roles-permissions) spell out who can change what.

## Tips

* **Pick the framework your team already speaks.** If your weekly review uses OKR language, pick OKR — don't relearn the framework just because Hamster supports six.
* **Start with one active framework, not five.** Frameworks compound; teams that run two in parallel rarely keep both fresh.
* **Use leading + lagging KPIs in OGSM.** A measure marked `leading` reports early; a `lagging` one reports outcomes. Mixing them is how strategies stay honest.
* **Link [Initiatives](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/initiatives) early.** A goal without linked [Initiatives](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/initiatives) is a wish; an [Initiative](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/initiatives) without a linked goal is unanchored work.
* **Don't over-nest.** OGSM tops out at four levels by design; OKR is two. Resist the temptation to add depth — the framework is enforcing what works.
* **Switch frameworks without ceremony.** Archiving an OKR framework and starting an OGSM is a five-minute operation; your [Initiatives](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/initiatives) and [Briefs](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/briefs) are untouched.
