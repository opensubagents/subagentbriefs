# Methods Library

Start with proven frameworks instead of a blank page. The [Methods Library](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/skills-methods/methods-library) gives your workspace pre-built [Methods](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/skills-methods) and skills you can activate, adapt, and make your own.

## Overview

Building a shared understanding of how your team works takes time. The [Methods Library](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/skills-methods/methods-library) shortens that by shipping a curated set of [Methods](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/skills-methods) — complete with their bundled skills — that you can turn on in one click.

Once active, a library [Method](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/skills-methods) works the same way as any [Method](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/skills-methods) you've created yourself: Hamster references it when helping you write [Briefs](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/briefs), generate [Plans](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/plans), and guide delivery. Your team gets the benefit of a structured process from day one, without the overhead of authoring it from scratch.

Every [Method](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/skills-methods) in the library is maintained by Hamster and stays current as best practices evolve. When you want to go further, you can fork any library [Method](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/skills-methods) into a version your team owns and can edit freely.

_This feature may need to be enabled for your workspace._

## What's in the Library

Two [Methods](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/skills-methods) ship in the initial library:

**Hamster [Method](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/skills-methods)** — the [Delivery](https://tryhamster.com/docs/concepts/delivery) process the Hamster team uses to ship products. Three bundled skills covering how to write [Briefs](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/briefs), structure [Delivery](https://tryhamster.com/docs/concepts/delivery), and capture outcomes. A good starting point if you want to align your team around the Hamster way of working.

**GStack** — Gary Tan's (Y Combinator) startup playbook, structured as a [Method](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/skills-methods) with 11 bundled skills. Covers the practices behind fast-moving, founder-led product teams: customer [Discovery](https://tryhamster.com/docs/concepts/discovery), tight [Briefs](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/briefs), direct feedback loops, and high-velocity delivery. Activate it to give Hamster the context it needs to guide your team like a YC-backed startup.

## How It Works

1. **Open the Library** — Navigate to Library in your workspace sidebar. You see all available [Methods](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/skills-methods), each with a description and its bundled skills listed.
2. **Enable a [Method](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/skills-methods)** — Toggle a [Method](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/skills-methods) on to make it active for your workspace. Hamster immediately starts referencing it when you ask for help writing [Briefs](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/briefs) or generating plans. You can enable as many [Methods](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/skills-methods) as are relevant to how your team works.
3. **Disable a [Method](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/skills-methods)** — Toggle a [Method](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/skills-methods) off to remove it from Hamster's context. The [Method](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/skills-methods) stays in the library; you can re-enable it at any time.
4. **Preview bundled skills** — Click any [Method](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/skills-methods) card to see its full skill list, even if the [Method](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/skills-methods) is currently disabled. This lets you understand what you're getting before switching it on.
5. **Fork a [Method](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/skills-methods)** — When you want to customise a library [Method](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/skills-methods), click **Fork**. Hamster creates a copy in your workspace that you fully own — you can edit every document, rename skills, and diverge as far as you need. Your fork is independent: updates Hamster makes to the library [Method](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/skills-methods) do not affect your copy.
6. **Find your fork** — After forking, the [Method](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/skills-methods) appears in your [Methods](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/skills-methods) list with a **from Library** badge so you can tell it apart from your bespoke methods. From there, treat it like any other [Method](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/skills-methods): open it in the editor, add child documents, adjust the skills.

## Key Capabilities

* **One-click activation**: Enable a library [Method](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/skills-methods) and Hamster starts using it as context immediately — no configuration, no copy-pasting.
* **Bundled skills included**: Each library [Method](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/skills-methods) comes with pre-written skills. When you enable a [Method](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/skills-methods), its skills become active in your workspace automatically.
* **Fork to customise**: Library [Methods](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/skills-methods) are read-only by design, so the shared source stays consistent. Fork creates your own editable copy, complete with all the bundled skills, without touching the original.
* **Conflict-free naming**: If a library skill's name matches something you've already created in your workspace, Hamster renames the forked copy automatically — you see a confirmation in the notification so nothing gets silently overwritten.
* **Stays current**: The library is maintained by Hamster. When content improves, the library version updates — your enabled (non-forked) [Methods](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/skills-methods) reflect the latest version. Forked copies are yours and do not receive library updates.

## Tips

* Enable GStack if your team wants to operate at founder speed. It gives Hamster a rich picture of how fast-moving product teams work, which produces sharper [Briefs](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/briefs) and more focused plans.
* If you enable a library [Method](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/skills-methods) and later decide to customise it, fork it first. Edits aren't possible on library [Methods](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/skills-methods) directly — forking takes a few seconds and gives you full control.
* Disabling a library [Method](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/skills-methods) doesn't remove it from the library. It just tells Hamster not to reference it for now. You can re-enable it any time.
* Use the skill preview on each library card to check what's bundled before enabling. Some [Methods](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/skills-methods) include foundational skills you may already have under different names — if so, fork and rename rather than duplicating.
