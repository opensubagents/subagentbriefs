# Skills Overview

_Skills capture individual capabilities your team has — the specific things you know how to do that Hamster Studio can reference when guiding your work._

## Overview

A skill represents a focused, named capability: something like "User Story Writing," "Code Review," or "Journey Mapping." Skills are discrete — they do not branch into sub-documents. They carry a title, a description, a category, and an active or inactive state.

![Skills library showing team capabilities](https://tryhamster.com/_site/images/features/skills-methods/skills-overview/skills-list-view-desktop-light.webp?v=6)![Skills library showing team capabilities](https://tryhamster.com/_site/images/features/skills-methods/skills-overview/skills-list-view-desktop-dark.webp?v=6) 

When you add skills to your workspace, Hamster Studio builds a picture of what your team is capable of. That context feeds into the AI assistant when it helps you [Plan](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/plans) work, write [Briefs](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/briefs), or think through delivery. The more accurately your skills reflect what your team does, the more relevant the guidance you get back.

Skills are separate from [Methods](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/skills-methods) but can be linked to one. If a skill belongs to a broader process — for example, "Wireframing" belonging to a "Design Sprint" [Method](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/skills-methods) — you can associate them during creation. Skills bundled with a library [Method](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/skills-methods) appear on your Skills page automatically when you enable the parent [Method](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/skills-methods) — see [Methods Overview](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/skills-methods/methods-overview) for the [Methods](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/skills-methods) Library.

_This feature may need to be enabled for your workspace._

## How It Works

1. Open the Skills tab — Navigate to Skills in your workspace sidebar. You see all skills currently in your workspace, displayed as cards or a table depending on your display preference.
2. Add a skill — Click "Add Skill" to open the creation dialog. You give the skill a title, an optional description, an optional category, and an optional [Method](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/skills-methods) association.
3. Activate or deactivate — Each skill card has a toggle switch. Active skills are available to Hamster Studio; inactive ones are stored but not referenced. New skills default to active.
4. Edit a skill — Click on a skill card to open its full document view, where you can write richer content using the editor. You can also open the edit dialog from the card's overflow menu to update the title, description, or category. If a skill was added from a template, editing it creates your own customized version.
5. Remove a skill — Use the overflow menu on any card to remove a skill from your workspace permanently.

## Key Capabilities

* **Card and table views**: Switch between a card grid and a compact table using the Display button. Your preference is saved per page.
* **Search and filter**: Use the search field to find skills by title or description. Use the Category dropdown to narrow to a specific domain.
* **Group by**: You can group your skill list by category, active/inactive status, or source (Hamster template vs. your own custom entry).
* **Sort order**: Sort by newest, oldest, or alphabetically. The sort preference is saved per page.
* **Category labels**: Each skill can be assigned one of six categories — Product, Ops, Experience, Development, Marketing, or Workflows. Categories are color-coded throughout the interface.
* **[Method](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/skills-methods) association**: When creating a skill, you can link it to an existing method. The skill then shows a "Linked [Method](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/skills-methods)" badge in its detail view.
* **Template source indicator**: Cards show whether a skill came from a Hamster-provided template (globe icon), is a library skill bundled with a [Method](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/skills-methods) (locked, "from Library" badge), or was customized by your team (pen icon).
* **Recommendations**: When you have fewer than eight active skills, the page surfaces a "Recommended" section showing templates from categories you already use.

## Library Skills

Hamster ships pre-built [Methods](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/skills-methods) through the [Methods Library](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/skills-methods/methods-library) at `/library`, and each library [Method](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/skills-methods) comes with a bundle of skills. When you enable a library [Method](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/skills-methods) for your account, every bundled skill automatically appears on your Skills page — no separate import step.

| State on the Skills page                                                                            | What you can do                                                                                                                  |
| --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Library skill (parent [Method](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/skills-methods) enabled)  | Read-only. Locked editor, "from Library" badge. The skill is active and reachable by the AI.                                     |
| Library skill (parent [Method](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/skills-methods) disabled) | Hidden from the Skills page. Re-enable the [Method](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/skills-methods) to bring it back. |
| Forked library skill                                                                                | Your copy. Fully editable. Lives under your account, marked as a fork.                                                           |

Forking the parent [Method](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/skills-methods) copies the bundled skills along with it — your forks are yours to edit, and the original library cards stop competing for the same name. If two skills would collide on name, Hamster appends a clarifying suffix on the fork rather than refusing the operation.

## Importing Skills from GitHub

You can import skills directly from any GitHub repository. From the Skills tab, click **Add Skill** and select **Import from GitHub**. Enter the repository URL, and Hamster scans it for skill files (SKILL.md with frontmatter). Select the skills you want to import, and they are added to your workspace with source attribution preserved.

Imported skills show a GitHub icon indicating their origin. You can edit imported skills to customize them for your team.

## CLI Skills Sync

The `hamster skills sync` command keeps skills in your repository in sync with your Hamster workspace. Run it from your project directory to:

* **Push** local skill files to Hamster
* **Pull** skills from Hamster into your repo
* **Detect conflicts** when both sides have changes

This is useful for teams that version-control their skills alongside code. The [CLI](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/cli) compares file content and asset files, showing actionable [Results](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/results) sorted by what needs attention.

## Editing Skill Frontmatter

Each skill supports markdown frontmatter for structured metadata — title, description, and category. You can edit frontmatter directly in the built-in editor. Changes to the category field update the skill's categorization across the interface immediately.

## Bulk Skill Management

You can enable or disable multiple skills at once from a [Method](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/skills-methods)'s Skills tab. Toggle the [Method](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/skills-methods) switch to bulk-enable all associated skills, or use the select-all control to manage skills in batch.

## Tips

* Deactivate skills that no longer reflect how your team works rather than removing them. That way you preserve the history without cluttering the active list.
* If your team uses skills differently from the template description, choose "Edit (create your version)" from the overflow menu. Your customized version replaces the template text for your workspace only.
* Use the Category filter when you want a quick audit of what is covered in a particular domain — for example, checking whether your Experience skills are complete before starting a design sprint.
* To customize a library skill, fork the parent [Method](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/skills-methods) (`/library` → Fork). The skill becomes yours under your account and the locked library card steps out of the way.
