# Discovery — figuring out what to do

[Discovery](https://tryhamster.com/docs/concepts/discovery) takes a [Goal](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/goals), [Initiative](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/initiatives), customer signal, or product hunch and turns it into [Brief](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/briefs)\-level context. You can test the hunch in code, ideate in Figma, whiteboard with your team, or pull the AI into [Hamster Chat](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/chat). Whatever the surface, [Discovery](https://tryhamster.com/docs/concepts/discovery) ends with an aligned [Brief](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/briefs) that can cross into [Delivery](https://tryhamster.com/docs/concepts/delivery).

This is where the 70% becomes possible to push toward 99%. The AI can draft something plausible from a prompt, but [Discovery](https://tryhamster.com/docs/concepts/discovery) gives it the user need, constraints, evidence, and acceptance criteria that make the last 30% specific to your team.

## Hamster chat makes a great design partner

[Hamster Chat](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/chat) is a persistent canvas where you talk to the AI assistant and your team at the same time, with the [Context Graph](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/context-graph) doing the lookup behind every reply.

Describe a hunch — _"We're hearing first-time buyers drop off at the checkout step. What do we know about that?"_ — and Hamster pulls in the relevant [Blueprint](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/blueprints), recent Linear tickets, customer-call mentions, prior [Briefs](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/briefs), and whatever else is connected, then replies with structured thinking. You ask follow-ups, push back, request a deeper pass, and pull in a teammate. The thread is the workspace; the AI is grounded in your team's real systems, not generic.

[Hamster Chat](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/chat) is not only a wrapper around an LLM. It is where ambiguity gets sharpened against everything your team has accumulated, in one room, with the AI as a participant. When the picture is clear, you ask Hamster to turn the conversation into a [Brief](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/briefs), and the first draft already references what [Hamster Chat](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/chat) surfaced.

## Research Agents — a structured pass

[Research Agents](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/research-agents) are a deeper, structured [Discovery](https://tryhamster.com/docs/concepts/discovery) pass for when a quick [Hamster Chat](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/chat) thread isn't enough. You hand off a question — _"Map our last six churn-cohort customer interviews against the activation funnel"_ — and the [Research Agent](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/research-agents) does the legwork across the [Context Graph](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/context-graph) and any external sources you allow.

Use [Research Agents](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/research-agents) when you're scoping an [Initiative](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/initiatives), validating an assumption, or trying to understand a customer signal across the data your team has already accumulated. The output lands as a structured document you can attach to a [Brief](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/briefs), so the work the [Research Agent](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/research-agents) did stays connected to the work that ships.

## The Brief during refinement

A [Brief](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/briefs) starts as a hunch in [Discovery](https://tryhamster.com/docs/concepts/discovery) and stays here while it's being refined. Scope clarifies, the [Blueprint](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/blueprints) comes along as context, alignment votes get collected. The same artefact later crosses into [Delivery](https://tryhamster.com/docs/concepts/delivery), which is why every shipped PR can trace back to the conversation that started it.

Refinement is the rest of [Discovery](https://tryhamster.com/docs/concepts/discovery) once a draft exists: sharpening scope, clarifying acceptance, gathering input from anyone who needs to weigh in. The AI assistant pushes back when scope is vague; you attach Figma frames, customer recordings, related [Briefs](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/briefs), and the AI uses them as it tightens the draft. If refining the [Brief](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/briefs) surfaces something the [Blueprint](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/blueprints) missed, the [Blueprint](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/blueprints) update is proposed automatically — [Knowledge](https://tryhamster.com/docs/concepts/knowledge) stays current as [Discovery](https://tryhamster.com/docs/concepts/discovery) happens.

When the [Brief](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/briefs) is good enough that humans and the AI both know what's being shipped, alignment votes go in, a [Plan](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/plans) generates, and the [Brief](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/briefs) crosses into [Delivery](https://tryhamster.com/docs/concepts/delivery).

## When Discovery ends

[Discovery](https://tryhamster.com/docs/concepts/discovery) ends when the [Brief](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/briefs) is good enough that humans and AI both know what's being shipped. The team votes the [Brief](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/briefs) ready, a [Plan](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/plans) generates, and the work crosses into [Delivery](https://tryhamster.com/docs/concepts/delivery).
