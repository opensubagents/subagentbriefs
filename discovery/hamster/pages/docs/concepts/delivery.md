# Delivery — what you ship to move a Goal

[Delivery](https://tryhamster.com/docs/concepts/delivery) is where aligned context becomes shipped work. [Discovery](https://tryhamster.com/docs/concepts/discovery) sharpens the bet; [Delivery](https://tryhamster.com/docs/concepts/delivery) turns that bet into [Initiatives](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/initiatives), [Briefs](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/briefs), [Plans](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/plans), [Tasks](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/tasks), and PRs that move a [Goal](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/goals).

[Delivery](https://tryhamster.com/docs/concepts/delivery) is also where the 70% to 99% loop closes. The AI does not start from a loose prompt; it starts from a [Brief](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/briefs), reads [Blueprints](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/blueprints) and [Methods](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/skills-methods), follows a [Plan](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/plans), executes [Tasks](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/tasks), and leaves a PR your team can review.

## Initiatives — committing to a Goal

An [Initiative](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/initiatives) is how a [Goal](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/goals) becomes committed work. Each [Initiative](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/initiatives) says which [Goal](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/goals) you're serving, who owns it, and how it's tracking — a clear line of sight from outcome to workstream.

[Initiatives](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/initiatives) are where outcome talk becomes ownership talk. A [Goal](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/goals) says "Activation should move from 30% to 60%". An [Initiative](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/initiatives) says "Onboarding redesign, owned by Priya, targeting +20pp by end of Q2, tracking on time." When [Briefs](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/briefs) roll up under an [Initiative](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/initiatives), you stop talking to your CEO about tickets and start talking about the bet you've committed to and whether it's working.

An [Initiative](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/initiatives) can serve more than one [Goal](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/goals) (with optional weights), and a [Goal](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/goals) can be served by more than one [Initiative](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/initiatives) — the link is many-to-many. State and health are tracked on the [Initiative](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/initiatives) itself, and the bidirectional [Linear](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/connections/linear) and [Jira](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/connections/jira) sync keeps the existing rituals (stand-ups, sprint reviews) intact.

## Briefs — capturing the change

A [Brief](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/briefs) is the unit of "we're building _this_" inside an [Initiative](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/initiatives). It captures what's being built — the [Goal](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/goals), the user need, the scope, the constraints, the design, the acceptance criteria — refined against the relevant [Blueprints](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/blueprints) until humans and AI both agree on what done means.

Most teams don't have one place where the next thing being built lives. They have a Linear ticket, a Notion spec, a Figma file, a Slack thread, and an engineer's interpretation — five sources, none complete, all hand-translated when an AI agent or a new engineer picks the work up. A [Brief](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/briefs) consolidates that into one English document that the team and the AI both read from. Stakeholders comment on the [Brief](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/briefs), the [Plan](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/plans) derives from the [Brief](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/briefs), the PR ships from the [Brief](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/briefs).

The [Brief](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/briefs) is also the through-line from [Discovery](https://tryhamster.com/docs/concepts/discovery) to [Delivery](https://tryhamster.com/docs/concepts/delivery). It doesn't start in [Delivery](https://tryhamster.com/docs/concepts/delivery) — it starts as a hunch in [Hamster Chat](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/chat), gets refined against the relevant [Blueprints](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/blueprints), and crosses into [Delivery](https://tryhamster.com/docs/concepts/delivery) when the team votes the [Brief](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/briefs) ready and a [Plan](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/plans) generates.

## Plans and Tasks — turning the Brief into work

A [Plan](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/plans) is a structured breakdown derived from the [Brief](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/briefs): [Tasks](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/tasks), subtasks, dependencies, and acceptance criteria. The [Plan](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/plans) is mostly internal scaffolding — it gives the AI agent (and any human shipping the work) a deterministic path from [Brief](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/briefs) to PR.

You don't manage a [Plan](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/plans) the way a PM would in a project tool. You glance at it once before [Delivery](https://tryhamster.com/docs/concepts/delivery) to make sure the agent understood the scope. If the [Plan](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/plans) misses something, you ask in [Hamster Chat](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/chat) and Hamster regenerates it; no hand-editing required.

[Tasks](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/tasks) are what actually runs. The agent walks the [Task](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/tasks) list in priority and dependency order, marks each [Task](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/tasks) as it goes, and the run streams into the [Brief](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/briefs)'s [activity timeline](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/briefs/activity-timeline) so you can see where the work is at any moment.

Stacked together, the layers go from outcome at the top to shipped change at the bottom:

```
Goal           ← Direction
Initiative     ← what we're going to ship
Brief          ← a deliverable chunk
Plan → Tasks → PR

```

## How work actually ships

Two paths run from the same [Brief](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/briefs):

* [Cloud Agents](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/cloud-agents) when you want hands-off [Delivery](https://tryhamster.com/docs/concepts/delivery). The [Plan](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/plans) goes to a [Cloud Agent](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/cloud-agents) and a PR comes back. Best for PM-kicked deliveries, [Routines](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/routines), and parallel work across the team.
* [CLI](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/cli) or [MCP Server](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/mcp) when you want full control with full context. Engineers stay in Cursor, Claude Code, Codex, or anything else that speaks MCP, pulling the [Brief](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/briefs), its [Plan](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/plans), the relevant [Blueprints](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/blueprints), and your connected context straight into their session.

Both paths read from the same [Briefs](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/briefs), [Plans](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/plans), and [Blueprints](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/blueprints), so you can switch on a per-[Brief](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/briefs) basis depending on what suits the work. Most teams use both — [CLI](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/cli) and [MCP Server](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/mcp) for individual engineers who want to be in the loop on the agent's choices, [Cloud Agents](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/cloud-agents) for everything else.
