# Solutions to common challenges

These guides start from the problems teams have described to us, they might sound uncomfortably familiar.

Hamster means fewer hours spent chasing alignment; product context that survives handoffs; AI agents that remember what your team already decided; specs that help the team build the right thing, not 400 more pages of the same process.

[Alignment overhead is eating the weekWhen review, sign-off, and status work crowd out actual product thinking.](https://tryhamster.com/docs/solutions/alignment-overhead)[The work about the work gets in the wayWhen the PM job becomes translating the same intent into docs, tickets, updates, and follow-ups.](https://tryhamster.com/docs/solutions/work-about-the-work)[Context leaves with teammatesWhen product nuance, architecture, and past decisions live in people instead of the team.](https://tryhamster.com/docs/solutions/context-walks-out-the-door)[AI has to be re-taught every sessionWhen every new Claude Code, Cursor, or Codex session starts with another manual context dump.](https://tryhamster.com/docs/solutions/reteach-ai-every-session)[Specs are the bottleneck, not buildingWhen AI made implementation faster, but deciding what to build still takes the week.](https://tryhamster.com/docs/solutions/specs-are-the-bottleneck)[Small changes after build are too expensiveWhen follow-up fixes require rediscovering stack context, deployment constraints, and old decisions.](https://tryhamster.com/docs/solutions/post-build-changes-are-costly)[Your tools do not share contextWhen briefs, initiatives, epics, docs, and conversations all live in different systems.](https://tryhamster.com/docs/solutions/fragmented-tools)[AI is making more artifacts, not better decisionsWhen the old process gets automated instead of replaced with clearer product delivery.](https://tryhamster.com/docs/solutions/ai-more-artifacts)[Rolling Hamster out safelyWhen the individual champion gets it, but the team needs integrations, permissions, security, and onboarding.](https://tryhamster.com/docs/solutions/adoption-and-governance)

## The break is between signal and shipped software

These problems look different on the surface. Underneath, the same thing keeps happening: customer signal, product judgment, team decisions, and delivery context get separated before the work ships.

Hamster keeps the chain intact:

**Signal → Brief → Plan → Tasks → Delivery → Blueprint**

The [Brief](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/briefs) carries the thing the team is agreeing to build. The [Plan](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/plans) and [Tasks](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/tasks) make it executable. [Delivery](https://tryhamster.com/docs/concepts/delivery) sends the work to an AI agent or the tools your engineers already use. The [Blueprint](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/blueprints), [Methods](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/skills-methods), and [Context Graph](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/context-graph) make the next loop start smarter than the last one.
