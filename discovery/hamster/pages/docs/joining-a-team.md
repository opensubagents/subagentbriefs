# Joining a team on Hamster

Welcome — someone on your team invited you to Hamster. This guide gets you oriented in about 10 minutes.

This guide:

* Is tailored for: individual contributors joining an existing Hamster workspace. Product managers, engineers, engineering managers, designers, and business stakeholders.
* And covers: what Hamster is, how to set up your account, what to do first based on your role, and where to go next.

> Setting up Hamster for your team from scratch? See the admin guides instead: [Small teams](https://tryhamster.com/docs/for/small-teams) · [Startups & mid-size](https://tryhamster.com/docs/for/startups-and-mid-size) · [Enterprise & scaling](https://tryhamster.com/docs/for/enterprise-and-scaling).

## What Hamster is

Hamster is the place your team's organisational understanding lives — what your products are, what's being built next, how your team works — in a structured English layer that AI can read. It sits on top of GitHub and Linear or Jira and the rest of your stack; it doesn't replace them.

If you want the full mental model, see the [concepts overview](https://tryhamster.com/docs/concepts). The short version:

* [Blueprints](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/blueprints) capture state — what your product is today.
* [Briefs](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/briefs) capture change — what's being built next, refined against the Blueprint.
* [Methods](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/skills-methods) capture ways of working — how AI helps your team consistently.
* Your [Context Graph](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/context-graph) connects all three to your existing tools.

For the conceptual model behind these artefacts, see [Concepts](https://tryhamster.com/docs/concepts) — [Delivery](https://tryhamster.com/docs/concepts/delivery), [Discovery](https://tryhamster.com/docs/concepts/discovery), [Direction](https://tryhamster.com/docs/concepts/direction), and Knowledge.

You'll mostly interact with [Briefs](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/briefs) day-to-day. Reading the four concept pages takes about 15 minutes and saves a lot of "what does this thing actually do?" later.

## Set up your account

1. Accept the invite. Click the link in your email and sign up using the same email address.
2. Step through onboarding. It's a couple of minutes. Hamster pulls your team's identity from your company URL, so most fields auto-populate.
3. Install the [Slack bot](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/connections/slack). Most teams use Slack as the primary notification surface. Notifications, alignment requests, and [Brief](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/briefs) side panels all land where you already work.
4. Optional: set up [Voice](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/chat/voice). If you want hands-free [Brief](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/briefs) writing while you walk or commute.

## What to do first, by role

Hamster's surface area is wide; what's relevant on day one depends on what you do on the team. Pick the section below that fits.

### If you're a Product Manager

Open a [chat](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/chat) and describe a small piece of work you'd normally write a [Brief](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/briefs) for. Let the AI help you draft it — answer its clarifying questions, attach any relevant context (Figma frames, customer call clips, screenshots, URLs), and let it produce a brief.

Once the [Brief](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/briefs) looks right, generate a [plan](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/plans/generating-plans). Vote alignment with a teammate (the [Brief](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/briefs) moves to "Aligned" status). Click **Deliver**. A PR appears in GitHub.

That's the loop end-to-end. Read [Briefs](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/briefs) and [Plans](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/plans) when you have 20 minutes for depth.

### If you're an Engineer or Engineering Manager

You stay in your IDE. Hamster's job is to give your existing AI coding tool — Claude Code, Cursor, Codex, whichever you already use — full access to your team's [Context Graph](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/context-graph), the [Brief](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/briefs) you're shipping, the relevant [Blueprints](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/blueprints), and the methods. You ship the way you already ship. The [Brief](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/briefs) just shows up with everything the agent needs to do the work.

1. Install the Hamster CLI. The [CLI](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/cli) gives you the [Brief](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/briefs) surface in your terminal — sync skills, run [slash commands](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/cli/slash-commands), trigger deliveries, scope contexts.
2. Wire up the [MCP Server](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/mcp). This is the part that matters. The [MCP Server](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/mcp) exposes Hamster's [Context Graph](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/context-graph), [Briefs](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/briefs), [Blueprints](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/blueprints), and [Methods](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/skills-methods) to your AI assistant. From Claude Code or Cursor, you can ask it to ship a specific [Brief](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/briefs) by name and the agent already has every piece of context it needs.
3. Skim the [Methods Library](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/skills-methods). The [Methods](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/skills-methods) are the playbook the AI reads from. If your team has forked it, knowing what's customized is useful — but you don't have to memorise it.
4. [Cloud Agents](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/cloud-agents) are optional. [Cloud Agents](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/cloud-agents) are how your admin gets [Briefs](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/briefs) delivered without an engineer's laptop in the loop — useful for deliveries kicked off by a PM or for background work. As an engineer, you don't have to use them. Most engineers ship from their IDE and that's intentional.

**Top tip:** "Ship the \[Brief title\] Brief" works as a one-liner from your AI assistant once the [MCP Server](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/mcp) is connected. You don't have to copy-paste the [Brief](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/briefs) contents anywhere.

### If you're a Designer

You don't need to write briefs. You attach to them.

1. Connect [Figma](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/connections/figma) to your account. Workspace-level [Connection](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/connections) but each individual user links their own Figma account.
2. Find a [Brief](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/briefs) your PM is drafting. Attach the relevant Figma frame or `.fig` file. The AI agent reads it as design context for the [Plan](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/plans) and delivery.
3. Watch the [activity timeline](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/briefs/activity-timeline) on [Briefs](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/briefs) you care about. It shows scope changes — including AI-driven ones — as they happen, so you don't get blindsided when a PR shows up that misses the design.

**Top tip:** Pin the design system source-of-truth (Figma file, Notion page, or Storybook URL) as a context document on every brief. AI agents respect what's in the [Brief](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/briefs); what's not in the [Brief](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/briefs), they invent.

### If you're a Business Stakeholder (Exec, Sales, CS, Ops)

You probably won't open Hamster directly day-to-day. Live in Slack.

1. Make sure the [Slack bot](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/connections/slack) is in your DMs. Notifications and [Brief](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/briefs) side panels land here.
2. Drop customer signal directly into Slack. Use `@Hamster` to mention the bot in any channel; paste a customer quote, a Gong clip URL, or a support ticket. The bot routes it to the right brief.
3. Use [slash commands](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/cli/slash-commands) to ask "what's shipping this week?" or "status of \[Initiative\]?" without leaving Slack.
4. Forward [public share links](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/briefs/sharing-briefs) to customers, advisors, or board members. They can read the [Brief](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/briefs) without a Hamster login.

## Tips for a fast first week

* **Don't try to learn all of Hamster at once.** The [Brief](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/briefs) surface plus Slack covers about 80% of day-to-day value. Adopt [Initiatives](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/initiatives), [Routines](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/routines), and [Methods](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/skills-methods) later.
* **Open chat first, not Notion.** Hamster's chat is the [Brief](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/briefs) writer. Pasting a finished doc loses the back-and-forth that makes [Briefs](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/briefs) better.
* **The AI reads everything you attach.** Figma frames, Gong clips, screenshots, support tickets, URLs. More grounding context = better [Briefs](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/briefs) and better deliveries.
* **The activity timeline is your friend.** If you're not sure what changed on a [Brief](https://tryhamster.com/docs/hamster-studio/briefs), look there before pinging Slack.
