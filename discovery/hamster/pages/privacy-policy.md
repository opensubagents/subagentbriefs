# Privacy Policy

## Last updated February 2026

## 1\. Introduction

Hamster is a technology company providing AI-powered services.

We understand that you are aware of and care about your own personal privacy interests, and we take that seriously. This Privacy Notice describes Hamster's policies and practices regarding its collection and use of your personal data, and sets forth your privacy rights. We recognize that information privacy is an ongoing responsibility, and so we will from time to time update this Privacy Notice as we undertake new personal data practices or adopt new privacy policies.

## 2\. Data Protection Officer

Hamster is headquartered in the United States. Hamster has appointed an internal data protection officer for you to contact if you have any questions or concerns about Hamster's personal data policies or practices. If you would like to exercise your privacy rights, please direct your query to Hamster's data protection officer at [privacy@tryhamster.com](mailto:privacy@tryhamster.com).

## 3\. How we collect and use (process) your personal information

Wheel Go Fast, Inc. collects personal information about its website visitors and customers. With a few exceptions, this information is generally limited to:

* name
* job title
* employer name
* work address
* work email
* work phone number

We use this information to provide prospects and customers with services. We do not sell personal information to anyone and only share it with third parties who are facilitating the delivery of our services.

From time to time, Wheel Go Fast, Inc. receives personal information about individuals from third parties. Typically, information collected from third parties will include further details on your employer or industry. We may also collect your personal data from a third party website (e.g. LinkedIn).

## 4\. Google API Services User Data

Hamster integrates with Google API Services to provide functionality that requires access to your Google account data. This section describes how we handle that data in compliance with the [Google API Services User Data Policy](https://developers.google.com/terms/api-services-user-data-policy), including the Limited Use requirements.

### 4.1 Data Accessed

When you connect your Google account, Hamster may request access to the following data depending on the features you use:

* **Google Drive** — File metadata, content, and folder structure.
* **Google Docs** — Document content and metadata.
* **Gmail** — Email metadata, message content, and attachments.
* **Google Calendar** — Event titles, descriptions, attendees, and scheduling details.
* **Google Photos** — Photo and video metadata and content.

In all cases, access is limited to the items you explicitly select or grant access to.

### 4.2 How We Use This Data

We use your Google data solely to provide the services you have requested:

* Indexing and retrieving content for use within Hamster's AI-powered features.
* Displaying your connected data within the application so you can interact with it.
* Processing your data through our AI services to provide insights, summaries, and other functionality you initiate.

We do not use Google data for advertising or to train general-purpose AI models.

### 4.3 Sharing

We do not sell your Google data. We share it with third parties only in these limited circumstances:

* **Service providers** — Cloud infrastructure and AI processing services that operate on our behalf to deliver Hamster's features. These providers are contractually prohibited from using your data for their own purposes.
* **Your consent** — When you explicitly share data with other users in your workspace (e.g., team members on a shared account).
* **Legal obligations** — When required by law, regulation, or enforceable governmental request.

### 4.4 Storage and Protection

Google data is stored on encrypted servers in the United States. We protect it with:

* Encryption in transit (TLS 1.2+) and at rest (AES-256).
* Access controls restricting data to authorized personnel and systems.
* Regular security assessments and infrastructure monitoring.
* Secure OAuth 2.0 token management for access and refresh tokens.

### 4.5 Retention and Deletion

We retain your Google data only as long as needed to provide the services you requested. When you disconnect your Google account or delete your Hamster account, we delete the associated Google data within 30 days. You can also:

* Disconnect your Google account from your Hamster settings at any time, which stops further access and triggers deletion of stored data.
* Revoke access directly from your [Google Account permissions page](https://myaccount.google.com/permissions).
* Request deletion at any time by emailing [privacy@tryhamster.com](mailto:privacy@tryhamster.com).

## 5\. Use of the tryhamster.com Website

As is true of most other websites, Hamster's website collects certain information automatically and stores it in log files. The information may include internet protocol (IP) addresses, the region or general location where your computer or device is accessing the internet, browser type, operating system and other usage information about the use of Hamster's website, including a history of the pages you view. We use this information to help us design our site to better suit our users' needs. We may also use your IP address to help diagnose problems with our server and to administer our website, analyze trends, track visitor movements, and gather broad demographic information that assists us in identifying visitor preferences.

Hamster has a legitimate interest in understanding how members, customers and potential customers use its website. This assists Hamster with providing more relevant products and services, with communicating value to our sponsors and corporate members, and with providing appropriate staffing to meet member and customer needs.

## 6\. Cookies and tracking technologies

Hamster makes available a comprehensive cookie notice that describes the cookies and tracking technologies used on Hamster's website and provides information on how users can accept or reject them. To view the notice, just click on our cookie policy link.

## 7\. Sharing information with third parties

The personal information Hamster collects from you is stored in one or more databases hosted by third parties located in the United States. These third parties do not use or have access to your personal information for any purpose other than cloud storage and retrieval. On occasion, Hamster engages third parties to send information to you, including information about our products, services, and events.

We do not otherwise reveal your personal data to non-Hamster persons or businesses for their independent use unless: (1) you request or authorize it; (2) it's in connection with Hamster-hosted and Hamster co-sponsored conferences as described above; (3) the information is provided to comply with the law (for example, compelled by law enforcement to comply with a search warrant, subpoena, or court order), enforce an agreement we have with you, or to protect our rights, property or safety, or the rights, property or safety of our employees or others; (4) the information is provided to our agents, vendors or service providers who perform functions on our behalf; (5) to address emergencies or acts of God; or (6) to address disputes, claims, or to persons demonstrating legal authority to act on your behalf. We may also gather aggregated data about our services and website visitors and disclose the results of such aggregated (but not personally identifiable) information to our partners, service providers, advertisers, and/or other third parties for marketing or promotional purposes.

The Hamster website connects with third party services such as Facebook, LinkedIn, Twitter and others. If you choose to share information from the Hamster website through these services, you should review the privacy policy of that service. If you are a member of a third party service, the aforementioned connections may allow that service to connect your visit to our site to your personal data.

The United States has not sought nor received a finding of "adequacy" from the European Union under Article 45 of the GDPR. Pursuant to Article 46 of the GDPR, Hamster is providing for appropriate safeguards by entering binding, standard data protection clauses, enforceable by data subjects in the EEA and the UK. These clauses have been enhanced based on the guidance of the European Data Protection Board and will be updated when the new draft model clauses are approved.

Depending on the circumstance, Hamster also collects and transfers to the U.S. personal data with consent; to perform a contract with you; or to fulfill a compelling legitimate interest of Hamster in a manner that does not outweigh your rights and freedoms. Hamster endeavors to apply suitable safeguards to protect the privacy and security of your personal data and to use it only consistent with your relationship with Hamster and the practices described in this Privacy Statement. For more information or if you have any questions, please contact us at [privacy@tryhamster.com](mailto:privacy@tryhamster.com)

## 9\. Data Subject rights

The European Union's General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and other countries' privacy laws provide certain rights for data subjects. Data Subject rights under GDPR include the following:

* Right to be informed
* Right of access
* Right to rectification
* Right to erasure
* Right to restrict processing
* Right of data portability
* Right to object
* Rights related to automated decision making including profiling

This Privacy Notice is intended to provide you with information about what personal data Hamster collects about you and how it is used.

If you wish to confirm that Hamster is processing your personal data, or to have access to the personal data Hamster may have about you, please contact us.

You may also request information about: the purpose of the processing; the categories of personal data concerned; who else outside Hamster might have received the data from Hamster; what the source of the information was (if you didn't provide it directly to Hamster); and how long it will be stored. You have a right to correct (rectify) the record of your personal data maintained by Hamster if it is inaccurate. You may request that Hamster erase that data or cease processing it, subject to certain exceptions. You may also request that Hamster cease using your data for direct marketing purposes. In many countries, you have a right to lodge a complaint with the appropriate data protection authority if you have concerns about how Hamster processes your personal data. When technically feasible, Hamster will—at your request—provide your personal data to you.

Reasonable access to your personal data will be provided at no cost. If access cannot be provided within a reasonable time frame, Hamster will provide you with a date when the information will be provided. If for some reason access is denied, Hamster will provide an explanation as to why access has been denied.

For questions or complaints concerning the processing of your personal data, you can email us at [privacy@tryhamster.com](mailto:privacy@tryhamster.com). Alternatively, if you are located in the European Union, you can also have recourse to the European Data Protection Supervisor or with your nation's data protection authority.

## 10\. Data storage and retention

Your personal data is stored by Hamster on its servers, and on the servers of the cloud-based database management services Hamster engages, located in the United States. Hamster retains service data for the duration of the customer's business relationship with Hamster and for a period of time thereafter, to analyze the data for Hamster's own operations, and for historical and archiving purposes associated with Hamster's services. Hamster retains prospect data until such time as it no longer has business value and is purged from Hamster systems. All personal data that Hamster controls may be deleted upon verified request from Data Subjects or their authorized agents. For more information on where and how long your personal data is stored, and for more information on your rights of erasure and portability, please contact us at: [privacy@tryhamster.com](mailto:privacy@tryhamster.com)
