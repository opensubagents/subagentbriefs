# Your backlog doesn't need to be groomed. It needs a brief.

Issue trackers track work. They capture the what — a title, a description, a status. But they don't capture the reasoning. The customer insight that prompted the work. The constraints the team discussed. The decision that ruled out the other approach.

Humans can ask questions and fill in the gaps. Agents can't — unless you arm them with information.

## Connect your issue tracker, and Hamster briefs it for you.

Link your Linear or Jira workspace. Hamster reads your tickets and writes a brief for each project — filling in the reasoning, customer context, and decisions that were never captured in the ticket.

The brief becomes the single source of truth. Your team aligns on it. The plan is generated from it. Agents execute against it. The ticket was just the starting point.

## Two-way sync keeps everything current.

Tasks created in Hamster sync to your issue tracker automatically. Status, priority, and assignee changes flow in both directions. Tickets close as tasks complete — no one touches them.

Your team and leadership stay informed in the tools they already use. When they ask how you've found another gear, invite them to Hamster.

## Transition at your own pace.

You don't need to rip out your issue tracker on day one. Hamster syncs both ways, so your existing workflows keep running while your team adopts a new way of working. Start with one project. Brief it. Generate a plan. Watch the tickets update themselves.
